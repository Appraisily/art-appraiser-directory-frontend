Frequently asked questions about Raleigh, North Carolina appraisers

How do I choose an art appraiser in Raleigh, North Carolina?

Look for professionals with ISA, ASA, or AAA credentials and USPAP-compliant reports. Start with our vetted directory, schedule a consultation, and ask about recent work that mirrors your collection.

What services do appraisers in Raleigh, North Carolina provide?

Specialists in Raleigh, North Carolina routinely assist with Estate settlement, Insurance appraisals, Charitable donations, Market analysis, Provenance research, and Antique appraisals. Many also support authenticity research, market analysis, and sale preparation for private collectors and institutions.

How much does an art appraisal cost in Raleigh, North Carolina?

Fees vary by scope. Local providers reference Hourly/project rates with 1-hour minimum; verbal consultations $150-$300/hour; travel fees apply beyond 20 miles, Contact for pricing information, and Project-based pricing (not explicitly specified). Expect a written report that complies with USPAP standards and documents methodology, comparables, and appraiser credentials.
